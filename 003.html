Preparing Pets





1. Determine If You Are Capable Of Caring For A Pet

While keeping pets is great fun, they are not easy to care for. All pets need time, money and affection. In addition, certain animals and breeds have various special needs. Make sure you want a pet in the long run.

  1. Some pets require special attention and care during the day. So, make sure you are home to care for pets.
  2. If you have children, find pets that are safe for children. For example, hamsters or fish would make great first-time pets.
  3. If you are planning to move or make a drastic lifestyle change, do not raise an animal until your life stabilizes






2. Choose A Pet That Fits Your Lifestyle

Even different types of dogs have different needs. So make sure you choose an animal that suits you. Do some research on the behavior and needs of the animal you like before buying. Don't just make one choice or breed immediately. Open your mind, and you may be surprised at the choices made.

Pay attention to the general needs of these pets:

  1. Dog. While dogs have many breeds, all types of dogs need attention, exercise, and enough space to roam.
  2. Cat. Smart and aloof, cats can be kept without much supervision. However, cats still need time and affection so they don't get fussy.
  3. Hamsters, Gerbils, Weasels and Rats. Rodents were suitable as first pets because they were economical and had a short life. However, these animals often smell strong.
  4. Fish. Fish need careful attention and care to survive. You will not be able to indulge in fish. Think of raising fish like tending a garden.
  5. Lizard. Independent and easy to care for, lizards will not offer much affection and can be difficult to diagnose when sick.
  6. Birds. Birds tend to be messy and are often noisy. Birds are also expensive and often temperamental, especially large birds such as parrots.




3. Prepare Your Home For Pets

Pets often cause problems if you don't create boundaries or safe spaces because they're curious, like to eat, and can't listen to your warnings. Birds can escape through windows, lizards can quickly run around houses, and dogs or cats can roam the streets. Pay attention to everything that your pet can't use to escape and make sure pet food is stored in a safe place that your pet can't reach.

  1. Remove dangerous objects, such as knives or poisoned food.
  2. If you want the animals to be able to play outside, install a fence in your yard.
  3. Prepare a room that will become a “bedroom” for the pet.
  4. Try to start raising animals when your life is quiet to avoid the stress of getting to know each other.




4. Prepare Supplies For Your Pet Before Keeping

Consult with pet store staff and assessment agencies about needs that need to be prepared, such as cages, toys, grooming supplies, etc. Finish your shopping before bringing your pet home. Teach family members how to use equipment so they can help one another. If you have young children, you can help them prepare for pet care by “feeding” dolls, or regularly watering plants.

Taking Care Of Pets





1. Prepare A Budget For Caring For Your Pet

The cost of raising animals is not exorbitant, but that doesn't mean you won't spend money. Following are the estimated annual costs for raising animals (based on ASPCA estimates):

  1. Dogs - $552.48 - $828.71 depending on size.
  2. Cats - $552.48
  3. Rabbit - $644.56
  4. Rodents - $276.24- $598.52 depending on size
  5. Fish - $36.83
  6. Small birds - $36.83
  7. Large birds (Parrots, Macau) - 644.56 - $1104.95

Set aside a few million for emergency vet fees.





2. Schedule Regular Visits To The Vet

Make sure you take your pet to the vet immediately after adopting it. Like humans, pets need checks for any problems before they become serious. Discuss how often you should schedule checkups, your pet's diet, and medical needs at your first visit to the vet.

  1. Make sure you schedule your injections and vaccines as soon as possible.
  2. Ask what symptoms to watch for if your animal is sick.
  3. Castration or sterilize your dog or cat to prevent your animal from breeding.
  4. Write down your vet's phone number as well as the veterinary hospital number for emergencies.




3. Make Sure Your Pet Is Getting The Right Food For Its Nutritional Needs

The cheapest food isn't necessarily the healthiest. Try not to feed your leftovers as human food contains substances and minerals that can be harmful to animals. Feed the appropriate food for your pet in the right portion.

  1. Do your research or ask your veterinarian about healthy food sources and portions.
  2. Natural foods, although they are more expensive, are healthier than dry or processed foods.




4. Give Your Pet Love And Care

While this is a must for dogs and cats, fish and lizards also need dedication and love to survive. Animals are social creatures like humans. Therefore, you should set aside time to play with your pets so that they get enough exercise and mental stimulation.

  1. Give the pet room to roam. The larger the animal, the more space it will need.
  2. Buy a toy or set of toys for you to play with together.
  3. Give occasional treats after your pet has behaved well. This way, motivated animals to behave well and good habits will be firmly embedded in your pet.




5. Clean Pets And The Environment

This will keep you healthy and happy. Create a regular cleaning schedule, at least once 2-3 weeks, and stick to it well. Clean pets and their living areas to prevent disease and bad odors.

  1. For larger animals such as cats and dogs, you can purchase a special cleaning container with a large tub and water hose.
  2. Take care of the animal's hair regularly, for example by brushing the hair or scrubbing the scales, once every few days.
  3. For dogs and cats, make sure you trim their nails to keep them short and not broken.




6. Do In-Depth Research On Your Pet

While this article provides general guidelines for pet employers, every animal is different and you will need to adjust to them accordingly. Ask friends who share pets, read books in libraries, and search online for discussion forums about your breed or pet species. Every knowledge gained will be valuable. Don't be too rigid when bringing your pet home. Animals also have personalities and will have different wants and needs.
