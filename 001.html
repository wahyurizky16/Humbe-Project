With their cheerful personality, cute behavior, and adorable faces, cats make the ideal pet. However, despite popular opinion, cats are not animals that need no care! To keep your cat healthy and cheerful, you need to know how to care for her and help create the best life for her.





Train the Cat to Urinate in Place

Encourage your cat to use its litter box. Most cats prefer the litter box to any other place in the house because of the texture of the litter used. However, there are still some steps that need to be taken to ensure that the litter box provided is the best place for your kitten to relieve themselves.

  1. Place the box in a quiet location so that the cat is not disturbed by people, dogs, or loud noises.
  2. To keep the litter box clean, make sure you pick up the litter every day and clean the box every week. Replace litter with new litter at least once a week.
  3. Provide multiple boxes if you have more than one cat. If you have two cats, keep three boxes available to place in different rooms in the house. One cat may try to "bully" another, weaker cat so that it can't use one litter box.




1. Make sure the litter box feels comfortable with the cat.

Don't scare or surprise your cat while he is using the box. If not, he will associate the box with a bad experience and avoid it. Buy a large box, even if you need to spend more. Cats feel more comfortable with a larger box (in terms of area, not height).

  1. Do not replace the used litter product with products from other brands because cats do not like sudden changes. Replacing the clay litter with a clumpy type of litter that can be picked up with a shovel (or vice versa) actually makes the pussy feel uncomfortable and stops using the litter box.
  2. Don't buy litter with a strong scent that might discourage you from using the litter box.




2. Consider the condition of a cat that is young or old

Keep in mind that kittens and older cats with arthritis or other health conditions may have a hard time getting in and out of boxes that are too tall. Use a box with a low wall and place it in a place that is easily accessible to kittens or cats with special needs. You can also buy a litter box with adjustable dimensions.





3. Provide claw poles

Scratching is normal behavior for cats and you cannot train them not to scratch. If the cat still has claws, he will need a paw pole or two to prevent him from scratching at furniture, wooden items, and other objects. By providing a claw post, you give him the opportunity to demonstrate normal, healthy cat behavior.





4. Do not allow the cat to come or explore a restricted surface or area

Cats are highly curious and will jump on to kitchen tables or other places where they may not be allowed. However, this behavior can be treated with a plastic rug (scat mat), a spray of water from a spray bottle, or a firm "no" warning. With time and patience, you can train him to stay away from protected areas. You can also use a tin of rattle (an empty soda can filled with a few pebbles, with the opening covered with adhesive tape). Drop the can carefully onto the floor to surprise her and away from any restricted surfaces or areas. DO NOT throw the can at the cat as this may jeopardize its safety.





5. Use cat pheromone products

These are available in the form of a spray or diffuser that plugs into an outlet and they spray out soothing synthetic pheromones. With this product, you can treat problems with bowel movements or improper scratching behavior. It has also been shown to soothe stressed or anxious cats.

Feed The Cat





1. Determine The Type Of Food To Be Given

Cat food is available in various types; Dry, semi-wet, and canned foods are the most commonly used types of food. Dry types of food can be stored easily and efficiently, but cats usually prefer the taste of semi-wet and canned food. Both types of food increase water intake in cats compared to dry food. In general, the type of food given ultimately depends on the cat owner's taste.





Sometimes, cats with certain medical conditions need more certain types of food. Consult the condition of the satisfied with the vet to get recommendations on the type of food that can be given.

2. Choose Foods With Quality Brands.

Like other animals, cats have specific nutritional needs. These animals are "true carnivores" that need animal protein to avoid serious health problems. Ask your vet for advice about a high-quality diet. Cheap cat food products may not contain enough nutrients to keep your cat healthy and happy.

  1. Look for food products that contain a lot of animal meat, such as beef, chicken, turkey, or fish.
  2.  Also, look for products that contain essential amino acids such as taurine and arginine, as well as fatty acids such as arachidonic and linolate.
  3.  Do not give your kitten human food unless you have received permission or a "green light" from the veterinarian. Certain types of human food can make cats seriously ill, or even poisonous (eg, chocolate).




3. Follow The Feeding Directions Given.

In general, cat feeding is based on the cat's age, weight, and activity level. Your cat usually eats fairly frequent small meals throughout the day. Ask your vet for recommendations on food products and methods of feeding if you feel confused.





4. Don't Give Too Much Food

Follow the vet's advice carefully and make sure your cat is moving or exercising a lot as obesity is one of the biggest health problems experienced by cats today. Obese cats have a greater risk of developing diabetes as adults. Being overweight can also lead to arthritis, heart problems, and other health problems in cats.
